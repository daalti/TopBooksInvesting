Warren Buffet Books for Investing in 2022

Who hasn't heard of Warren Buffett? He constantly ranks high on Forbes' list of billionaires and is one of the richest persons in the world. As of June 2022, his net worth was estimated to be $100 billion. Both as a shrewd businessman and a generous philanthropist, Buffett is well-known.

He is most well-known for being one of the most successful investors in the world. Because of this, it is not unexpected that Warren Buffett's investment approach has grown legendary in scope. Buffett adheres to a number of crucial principles and an investment philosophy that is widely accepted worldwide. What exactly are the keys to his success, then? Continue reading to learn more about Buffett's approach and how his investments have allowed him to accumulate such a wealth.

n 1930, Warren Buffett was born in Omaha. At a young age, he became interested in business and investment, including in the stock market. Buffett began his studies at the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School before returning home to attend the University of Nebraska, where he earned a bachelor's degree in business administration. Later, Buffett attended Columbia Business School and graduated with a master's in economics.

Early in the 1950s, Buffett started his career as a salesman of investments, but in 1956 he founded Buffett Associates. In 1965, less than ten years later, he took over as CEO of Berkshire Hathaway. Buffett declared his intention to give away his whole fortune to charity in June 2006.

The Giving Pledge initiative was then founded by Buffett and Bill Gates in 2010 to inspire other affluent people to explore philanthropy.

The sixth book on the list, "The Essays of Warren Buffett", was written by Lawrence Cunningham and compiles the key points from all of Berkshire Hathaway's annual shareholder letters. These letters were written by Buffett himself, and the book was released in 1997. Despite the book's 328 pages, there is a ton of content. Buffett discusses a wide range of topics, including what it takes to become a great investor, mergers and acquisitions, what a seedy place Wall Street is, how newspapers have evolved, human psychology, the failure of academics in finance, the value of contrarianism, and back to what a seedy place Wall Street is again.

The quality of Buffett's writing, teaching, and investing are evident throughout this book. Even when he makes an effort to explain his reasoning to a less knowledgeable audience, there are instances when it only demonstrates how much ahead of his intellect is and how challenging it may be to understand some of the concepts. Nevertheless, a fantastic novel. I should add that almost all of the Berkshire Hathaway annual shareholder letters can be found at https://berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html for free, but Lawrence Cunningham does a great job of distilling the information.
